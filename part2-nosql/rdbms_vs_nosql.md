## Database Recommendation

For a healthcare startup building a patient management system, I would recommend **MySQL** as the primary database. Healthcare data is highly sensitive and requires strong guarantees around data integrity, consistency, and reliability. Relational databases like MySQL follow **ACID (Atomicity, Consistency, Isolation, Durability)** principles, which ensure that patient records, medical histories, prescriptions, and billing data remain accurate and consistent even in the presence of failures or concurrent access. From a CAP theorem perspective, such systems typically prioritize **Consistency and Partition Tolerance (CP)** over Availability, which is appropriate in healthcare where incorrect or partial data can have serious consequences.

MongoDB, on the other hand, is more aligned with **BASE** properties (Basically Available, Soft state, Eventually consistent). While this provides flexibility and scalability, eventual consistency is usually not acceptable for core healthcare workflows like diagnosis updates, medication orders, or lab results. Regulatory requirements (such as HIPAA-like compliance concepts) also favor structured schemas and strong transactional guarantees, both of which are better supported by relational databases.

However, the recommendation may change slightly if the startup also needs to add a **fraud detection module**. Fraud detection often involves analyzing large volumes of semi-structured or unstructured data such as logs, user behavior patterns, and event streams. In this case, **MongoDB could be introduced as a complementary system** rather than a replacement. MongoDB’s flexible schema and horizontal scalability make it well-suited for storing and processing diverse data used in analytics and machine learning pipelines.

In conclusion, MySQL should be used as the system of record for patient management due to its ACID guarantees, while MongoDB can be leveraged alongside it for fraud detection and analytical workloads, forming a polyglot persistence approach.
